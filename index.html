Sikkim

Majid Hussain 09 June 2018 6 min read

They say sometimes the journey is more enthralling than the final destination. During the road trip to Sikkim a very small but diverse state, I can very confidently say that there was an intense competition between the two to outplay each other. The journey with fellow bikers marked by scenic landscapes of lush green Himalayas and pristine waterfalls, interesting but weird conversations and to top it all the perfect weather to ride will always reserve a special place in me. The cruising Royal Enfield was fueled by the enthusiasm of the riders managed to jump over every hurdle that threatened its momentum. From negotiating for permits to strict laws of the state, from fuel scare at 12000 feet to stony bumpy roads to Gurudongmar lake, to losing my balance for the first time in my biking career to having gained a friendships that I know will last a lifetime, the journey was as action packed as it could get. The thrills of journey were maximise by the beauty of the destinations. The urbane and classy Gangtok, the mystical and quite lachung valley, the frozen beauty of Gurudongmar lake, the colorful and snowy yumthang valley and the mammoth zuluk la pass, each of them way better than what we had imagine. The warming hospitality of the people of Sikkim deserves a special thank you, they were our guides and at times saviour on these new and confusing roads. To wind up, I know there have been and will be numerous road trips in life but biking trip to Sikkim will always have special place in our hearts the journey of six days has given memories that shall be cherished for lifetime.


Zuluk La:

Zuluk la, was an integral part of the silk route trade is abode of peace with a enchanting view of Kanchenjunga, only if zuluk could speak abt the stories it has witnessed! Nevertheless the place still has the ability to transmit you to an ancient era.


Padamchen:

A lush green village in eastern Sikkim with most heart warming homestay. Very unnaturally the quiteness of the place only elevates one's medley of thoughts, with starry night cover and warm bonfire padamchen is truly a thinker's Paradise.

South India Random Trip - Chikmangalur

Majid Hussain 24 May 2018 6 min read

Being an adventure chaser, I am always up for long bike rides. Such was the idea for a random trip with 12 amazing folks.It was a random trip among 12 strangers, who weren't strangers anymore after couple of days

It all started when Rufus Reynolds turned on the engine from Bombay(aka Mumbai) to meet Majid, Karteek Sivagouni and Linga at Hyderabad. We met the remaining gang at Hyderabad and Bangalore. All we had was couple of bikes, cars and hell lot of adrenaline rush. We drove towards the greenery of Chikmagalur and covered Agumbe, Shimoga and Jog Falls.
